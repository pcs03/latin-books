## Early American History {.unnumbered}

::: {lang=en-US}

### Christopher Columbus

:::

Quōdam in oppīdō Ītaliae ōlim nātus est puer, quī Columbus appellābātur. Diū in patris officīnā^[**officina** **officina, -ae** f., _workshop_.] labōrāvit. Sed prope erat mare, puerque saepe ad lītus ībat, ut vidēret nāvēs, quae ē portū ad terrās exībant dīversās. In nāvibus erant hominēs multī, et Columbus mare ipse trānsīre saepe voluit; tum autem pecūniam nūllam habēbat. Sed posteā, cum iam iuvenis esset, usque ad^[**usque ad:** _all the way to,_ lit. _even to._] Britanniam et Āfricam nāvigāvit.

Illīs temporibus^[**īllīs temporibus:** _in those days._] nautae timēbant mare Atlanticum, cursumque prope lītus tenēbant. Interdum^[**interdum:** not **interim**.] autem secundum Āfricae ōram^[**ōram:** _i.e._ **lītus** (ōra, -ae f.)] longē nāvigātum erat^[**nāvigātum erat:** _people had sailed,_ lit. _it has been sailed_ (impersonal passive).], quod Henrīcus, rēx Lūsitāniae, invenīre viam volēbat, quā^[**quā:** _by which;_ antecedent, **viam**] nāvēs circum Āfricam prōgressae, ad Asiam pervenīre possent^[**possent:** _could;_ subjunctive in a relative clause of purpose. In translating the verb **possum**, some other rendering than "be able" should often be chosen.].

Quīdam^[**quīdam:** masc. pl., used as a noun.] tum crēdēbant terram esse rotundam^[**rotundam** : **rotundus, -a, -um** _round,_ or _spherical_], Columbusque etiam spērāre coeperat se trānsīre mare Atlanticum posse, et ita ad Asiam pervenīre; nēmō enim intellegēbat terram tam magnam esse, nec^[**nec:** _and ... not_] Columbus ipse suspicātus est Americam interpōnī^[**interpōnī:** lit. _to lie between_].
